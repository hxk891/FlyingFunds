How this works
Each dot represents a randomly generated portfolio using your holdings, with weights that sum to 100%. The frontier is the upper-left edge — portfolios that maximise return for their level of risk. Max Sharpe ★ maximises return per unit of risk. Min Volatility ◆ minimises total risk. If showing simulated data, upload a historical price CSV in Settings for real correlations.

About this model
Uses Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM), the model underlying Black-Scholes option pricing. Each path follows S(t+dt) = S(t) · exp[(μ − σ²/2)dt + σ√dt · ε], where ε ~ N(0,1). Shaded bands show the 5th–95th percentile of outcomes across all paths. Monthly contributions are added every 21 business days. This is not financial advice. Real markets exhibit fat tails and regime changes not captured by GBM.

CAPM explained
The Capital Asset Pricing Model regresses your portfolio's daily returns against a market benchmark. β (Beta) measures market sensitivity. β=1 tracks the market, β>1 amplifies moves, β<1 is defensive. α (Alpha) is excess return after removing market exposure. Positive alpha signals risk-adjusted outperformance. R² shows what fraction of your portfolio's variance is explained by the benchmark. A high R² with low alpha suggests index-like behaviour; low R² indicates stock-specific (idiosyncratic) risk dominates.
